Q2. The IT-Specific and General skills required 

 

 

  Abdul – The required IT skill set for Cyber security analysts are SQL, JavaScript, Python, C, C++ and PHP. (Garbade. M 2018). These required skill sets rank SQL 1st, JavaScript 3rd, Python 4th and C, C++ and PHP are not listed in the top 10 software and programming skills in-demand from employers. The three highest ranked IT-specific skills which are not in Cyber security analysts’ required skill set are Java, LINUX and Systems Development. These rank 2nd, 5th and 6th accordingly.  

 

The required general skill set for Cyber security analysts are communication skills, problem solving, trouble shooting and creativity. These skills rank in 1st, 2nd, 6th and 9th respectively in terms of demand from employers. The three highest general skills which are not in the Cyber security analyst required skill set are planning, writing and research. These soft skills rank 3rd 4th and 5th accordingly.

Q3. Has your opinion of your ideal job changed? Why or why not? 

 

 

  Abdul – Although Cyber security analysts weren’t among the top 9 most in demand jobs in Australia, Abdul will continue to strive to complete his Bachelor of IT and become an analyst in his respective field. This is mainly due to the shortfall of cyber security professionals in years previous. “Australia is currently short of 2300 workers in cyber security, with an expected demand of at least 17,600 additional professionals in the sector by 2026.” (Institute of Data 2019). Abdul is pleased to learn that programming skills such as SQL, JavaScript and Python are high in demand by employers, it is also relevant to his ideal field. This helps him to pursue this career path. In terms of general skills, Cyber security analysts are expected to communicate, problem solve, trouble shoot and be creative. These exact traits are also highly sought after by employers in the IT industry and aligns with his ideal IT role. Abduls opinion – Cyber security will be a fun and challenging career, and with exponential job demand, the future is bright. Abdul will continue to pursue this particular career path because it plays on his interest and because of the fantastic remuneration.  

 

 

  Seungmin – In reference to the Burning Glass statistics, programmers were the 9th most in demand IT job in Australia. Despite Seungmin’s dream job not topping the list, she aims to become a professional in this area. Many visits to the arcade with her friends have led her to develop a love for everything games, from online games, to single players, console or PC gaming. Games has motivated her to make it into her career. Seungmin acknowledges that there will be challenges ahead in learning the necessary languages such as C, C# and C++ despite these skills not highly sought after by employers. Seungmin enjoys the fact that many professional skills in her ideal role – communication, problem-solving, detail orientation and creativity are highly sought after by employers in the industry. Despite Burning Glass placing her ideal occupation 9th place under “programming”, Seungmin’s opinion - Games and game development is my passion, and I want to make this passion into my career and will continue to do so, stays the same.  

 

 

  Anthony - After learning more about the IT industry and the role of System Administrators, Anthony has dramatically changed his opinion of his ideal job.   Brian – Brian's opinion of his ideal job as a software engineer has hardly changed. Brian’s opinion of the role Software engineer remains the same – extremely challenging but extremely rewarding, and will continue to strive for his ideal role in IT.

So, I'm not worried about future of this position, remains mostly unchanged.
